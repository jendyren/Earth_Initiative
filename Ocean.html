The Ocean

The Past

Before human interferance in the ocean became an issue, the oceans were completely self-regulated. Every species within them kept the others in check. Even those with no predators rejoined the food chain by supporting other animals once they passed on. Every ecosystem was rich, prosperous, and self-regulating. A Hawaii-based laborotory began to notice a spike in CO2in the atmosphere in 1958, 100 years after they had begun operations. Humans also began to dump their trash into the ocean with no care for the negative effects this may have on the environment, making obvious an ongoing trend of human apathy towards non-human entities.

The Present

Currently, we as humans are at a turning point. We're on the verge of an incredibly devastating event. If humans keep going the way we're going (overfishing, careless trash dumping, oil drilling, non-renewable resources, etc.), then by 2030, the world will be irreperably damaged. This doesn't mean that every living thing is going to die, no. This generation may even be able to lead partially normal lives. The issue will be for our children, and everyone who comes after us. At present, humans have been consistently exhibiting a blatant disregard for our oceans. 98% of CO2 emissions are absorbed by the ocean, causing it to heat up at a disproportionate rate compared to the air. The marine plant life, which produces 70% of our oxygen, cannot adapt fast enough, and is dying. We are also heavily overfishing.

The Future

In short, if the oceans die, we die. Thousands of species are on the verge of extinction due to human activity. Species whose absence would cause the ecosystem to collapse irreparably. Examples of this include Orange Roughies, a deep-sea species of schooling fish that humans have nearly wiped out in a matter of years due to their taste, and nearly every species of shark. These animals are part of the ecosystem for a reason. If we as humans don't control ourselves and help these species come back, we will go extinct along with them. Our careless trashing of the planet will lead to the death of the oceans, and subsequently the death of us.
