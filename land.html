The Land

The Future

"Our species caused 322 animal extinctions in the past 500 years, with two-thirds of those happening in the past two centuries (as of 2014)". These statistics are concerning for multiple reasons. Not only does it show a blatant disregard for the natural habitat and integrity of animals and nature, but it, combined with the human habit to introduce invasive species to foreign environments, shows a disturbing trend of the human love of "Playing God." If left unchecked, we will destroy our planet, and there will be nobody to blame but ourselves. At present, one of the more concerning dying species would have to be honeybees. Every species, no matter how insignificant or irritating they may seem to us, plays a critical role in the balance of the environment, and there's nothing we can do to fill that gap once those species are gone. If we keep going the way we're going, the planet is going to die, and it's going to take us with it.

The Present

At present, like with the ocean, we are at a critical point in the environment's destruction. Thousands of years after we began, we humans are still destroying the environment for our own gain. This includes but is not limited to poaching, overhunting, air pollution, regular pollution, trash dumping, and forest clearing to make way for framland. By being so destructive with the environment (especially places like the rainforests, which we clear out acres and acres of for wood), we drive species out of their natural habitats and into our own. The leads us to think of them as a pure nuicance and subsequently drive them into extinction. There's also our love of animal products such as fur, claws, horns, teeth, etc., which is feuling the poaching industry. We have a bad habit of killing animals and just assuming there's an endless supply of them. Of course, this is financially motivated. Additionally, treating nature as our personal trash dump, as pictured above.

The Past

Much like the ocean, with minimal human interferance, the land biomes are self-regulating. Before humans, there were many great extinctions and such, however everything was where it was supposed to be, and creatures were adapted for their home environments. That all changed with human involvement. Mostly beginning with the major empires of Europe and Asia, humans began to absolutely ravage the environment for all of the things that they desired. If that meant leveling a forest, so be it. If that meant poluting even their own air quality with coal dust and harmful greenhouse gasses, that was fine, too! It's all for the sake of advancement, so throughout history, our reckless trashing of the environment was justified in our minds. This began to become less acceptable as we realized that these actions had consequences.
